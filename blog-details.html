5 WAYS TO INCREASE YOUR GRIP STRENGTH

ALEX STONE

MARCH 28, 2024 • 8 MIN READ

THE FOUNDATION OF MODERN CLIMBING

Grip strength isn't just about how hard you can squeeze; it's about the biomechanical efficiency of your forearms and tendons. At Summit Ascent, we see many beginners focus solely on pull-up power, but the real secret lies in the fingers.

1. HANGBOARDING WITH INTENTION

The most effective tool for finger strength is the hangboard. However, overdoing it can lead to tendon injury. Start with 10-second hangs on large edges, ensuring your shoulders are engaged and your elbows slightly bent.

2. THE POWER OF PINCH GRIPS

Pinch strength is often neglected. Practice pinching two small plates together or use a specialized pinch block to challenge the thenar muscles of your hand.

"The best climbers are not those who are the strongest, but those who can hold onto the smallest edges with the most efficiency."

Continue practicing these techniques consistently, and you'll notice a massive difference in your ability to hold onto technical problems and overhanging lead routes.
